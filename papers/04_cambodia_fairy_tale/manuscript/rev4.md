### R6: Wedeen comparison compression (pp. 40–41)

**Location:** The passage beginning "The normalization framework finds a productive analogue in Wedeen's analysis..." through "...the erosion of the psychological infrastructure required to sustain dissent in the absence of institutional support." (pp. 40–41)

**Action:** Replace the entire passage with:

"The normalization framework finds a productive analogue in Wedeen's analysis of political compliance in Assad's Syria, where citizens enacted elaborate rituals of regime praise not from belief but because acting as if they believed was the condition of ordinary life (Wedeen 1998, 2000). The Cambodian case differs in a respect that is analytically fundamental. Wedeen's compliance is performative: citizens maintain private skepticism while publicly enacting belief. The Cambodian pattern visible in the ABS data is not performative in this sense. The survey responses are private, administered with confidentiality protocols. The attitudinal shifts appear to reflect genuine reorientation, not strategic concealment: citizens moved into conditional authoritarianism rather than into noncommittal safe responses, and the shifts cut across demographic subgroups in ways that fear-based models do not predict. What the cases share is the outcome — a population that has arranged its evaluative life around the available political reality — but through different mechanisms. Wedeen's Syrians performed accommodation while potentially preserving private dissent; the Cambodian data suggest something closer to the internalization of accommodation, or at minimum, the erosion of the psychological infrastructure required to sustain dissent in the absence of institutional support."
